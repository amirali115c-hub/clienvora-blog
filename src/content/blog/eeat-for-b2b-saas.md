Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust. E-E-A-T is not a ranking factor you can install. It is the quality bar Google and the AI engines use to decide whether your content is worth surfacing at all.

For B2B SaaS, where buying cycles are long and risk is high, E-E-A-T is the difference between a page that gets cited and a page that gets ignored.

## What Experience Looks Like on the Page

Experience is the hardest E to fake and the easiest to demonstrate. It shows up as first-hand detail: the specific dashboard you built, the migration you ran, the failure mode you hit and how you resolved it.

A post that says "many teams struggle with onboarding" is a claim. A post that says "we watched 40 mid-market logistics teams take 14 days to first value before we rebuilt the onboarding flow to 3 days" is experience. One is forgettable. The other is citable by a human and an AI engine alike.

## How B2B SaaS Sites Build the Signal

**Name your authors.** Anonymous "Admin" posts read as content farm output. A named author with a real role and a linked profile tells the parser there is a person accountable for the claim.

**Show the work, not just the result.** Case specifics, methodology, and honest numbers outperform polished vagueness. The credibility is in the detail.

**Build entity coherence.** Your site, your authors, and your organization should reference the same entities consistently across pages, in copy and in schema. Coherent entities are what let an AI engine treat you as a reliable reference rather than a one-off page.

## The Payoff

E-E-A-T is not a box to tick before publishing. It is the reason some B2B sites compound authority quarter over quarter while others publish into a flat line. The sites winning in 2026 are the ones that treated demonstrated experience as the core of the content strategy, not an afterthought to keyword research.
