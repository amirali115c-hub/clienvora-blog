The buyer who matters to you this quarter may never see your blue link. They asked Perplexity. They read a ChatGPT answer. They skimmed a Google AI Overview that cited three of your competitors and not you.

That is Generative Engine Optimization, or GEO. And for B2B distribution, it is the largest free channel most companies have not touched.

## What GEO Actually Changes

Traditional SEO optimized for a ranked list. GEO optimizes for inclusion inside a synthesized answer. The unit of success is not a position. It is a citation: did the engine name you, quote you, or link to you inside the response.

According to Seer Interactive's 2025 research, brands cited within AI Overviews earned 35 percent more organic clicks and 91 percent more paid clicks than brands that were absent. Previsible's 2025 AI Traffic Report measured a 527 percent jump in AI-referred sessions between January and May 2025 alone. This is not a side channel. It is a redistribution of discovery traffic that is already underway.

## The Three Things That Get You Cited

Getting quoted by an AI engine is not mysterious. It is a function of three content properties:

**Declarative structure.** Write sentences that stand alone as answers. "Our audit found topical cannibalization on 63 percent of client sites onboarded in Q1 2025" is extractable. "We drive better outcomes" is not.

**Machine-readable context.** JSON-LD schema that names the author entity, the Organization, and the publication date tells the parser what the page is before a human reads a word. Google's Rich Results Test validates this for free.

**Specific, attributable claims.** An AI system will not quote a vague superlative, because quoting it introduces attribution risk. It quotes facts it can defend. The more concrete your claims, the more citable you become.
